# Signals and Systems (EEE307)


There are 2 online courses:
- [MIT 6.003: Signals and Systems (Freeman)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-003-signals-and-systems-fall-2011)
- [MIT 6.007: Signals and Systems (Oppenheim)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/res-6-007-signals-and-systems-spring-2011/)

And 3 books:
- [Signals and Systems (Oppenheim)](https://annas-archive.gl/md5/8169478eeb6b45dc63f1165f8cb90681)
- [Discrete-time Signals and Systems (Freeman)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-003-signals-and-systems-fall-2011/7b3f66736bb503468103ce8a55fe16e0_MIT6_003F11_notes.pdf)
- [Linear Systems and Signals (Lathi)](https://annas-archive.gl/md5/4e15e058430c801865a53d6d9126dd02)

You will realize that "MIT 6.007" and the book "Signals and Systems (Oppenheim)" are the oldest ones, 1-1 mapping between the course and the book, taught in 1986.

The "Modern (Digital)" <u>MIT 6.003</u> first teaches from <u>Discrete-time Signals and Systems (Freeman)</u>, then completes the curriculum using <u>Signals and Systems (Oppenheim)</u>, but not according to its chapter order. The Prof. is trying to teach Discrete-time Signals, then show how they are very similar to Continuous-time Signals. This course also emphasizes the <strong>Block Diagram</strong>, <strong>Operator Representation</strong>, and <strong>System Functional</strong>. Read the preface of <u>Discrete-time Signals and Systems (Freeman)</u> to know why.

<u>Signals and Systems (Oppenheim)</u> was [hard to read](https://web.archive.org/web/20260324191608/https://redlib.catsarch.com/r/EngineeringStudents/comments/4kost0/comment/d3hf9qo/). It's like a reference book. <u>Linear Systems and Signals (Lathi)</u> was better for self-studying.

The curriclum in this website will be a mapping between <u>MIT 6.003</u> and the books <u>Discrete-time Signals and Systems (Freeman)</u> and <u>Discrete-time Signals and Systems (Freeman)</u>.
